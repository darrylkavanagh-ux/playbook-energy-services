## 2. LIABILITY ASSESSMENT

### A. The Case Against the Second Defendant (Chiropractor)
The claim here rests on *Montgomery v Lanarkshire Health Board* [2015] regarding informed consent.
*   **Strengths:** High-velocity neck manipulation carries a known (albeit rare) risk of arterial dissection. If the Plaintiff was not explicitly warned of the risk of stroke, the consent is vitiated.
*   **Weaknesses:** Proving the manipulation *caused* the dissection rather than the dissection being the reason for the visit (neck pain). However, the "thunderclap" headache onset immediately post-manipulation strongly favours the Plaintiff.
*   **Probability of Establishing Liability:** **65%**

### B. The Case Against the First Defendant (Hospital)
This aspect of the case is overwhelmingly strong.
*   **The Error:** Prescribing Sumatriptan to a patient with a dissecting artery is a fundamental breach. Sumatriptan constricts blood vessels. In a dissection, this constricts the collateral flow that is keeping the brain alive, precipitating the stroke.
*   **Res Ipsa Loquitur:** The prescription of a contraindicated drug is a "Never Event." The video evidence of the medication box is a "smoking gun."
*   **Causation:** The temporal link between taking the medication and the onset of the major stroke is likely to be proven by pharmacological evidence.
*   **Probability of Establishing Liability:** **90%+**
