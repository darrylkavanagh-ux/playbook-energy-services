## 5. TARIFF CLASSIFICATION ANALYSIS

**Finding:** The account has been incorrectly classified under the DG5 NSHeat tariff for approximately five years despite zero night storage heating consumption.

**Causal Chain:**

1. **Pre-2021:** Property operated as residential accommodation with night storage heating. NSHeat tariff classification was appropriate.

2. **~2021:** Property underwent commercial conversion to office accommodation. Night storage heating was removed or disconnected.

3. **2021-2026:** NSHeat tariff classification carried over despite change of use. Flogas failed to identify the misclassification.

4. **Evidence of Misclassification:** Five years of consistent zero consumption on the NSH meter (Z00002768) confirms no night storage heating operation.

5. **Supplier Obligation:** Energy suppliers have a regulatory obligation to review accounts with anomalous consumption patterns and ensure appropriate tariff classification.

**Regulatory Context:**
Under CRU regulations, suppliers must ensure customers are placed on the most appropriate tariff for their consumption profile. The persistence of zero NSH consumption over five years represents a clear anomaly that should have triggered tariff review.

**Financial Impact:**
The financial impact of tariff misclassification cannot be quantified without comparative rate data between DG5 NSHeat and standard DG5 commercial tariffs for the full period since conversion. This data has been formally requested from Flogas.

- **60 Lower Baggot Street:** Approximately 50% higher electricity consumption than portfolio average
- **Evidence Quality:** Direct same-company, same-use, same-area comparison provides the strongest possible benchmark

### 6.3 Meter Reading Analysis

**Current Meter Status:**
- Meter Z00006629 reading: 58,872 (as of 19/01/2026)
- Current consumption rate: ~200 kWh/day

**Historical Analysis:**
At the current verified consumption rate of approximately 200 kWh/day:
- 10 months of consumption would generate a reading of ~60,000
- 5 years of consumption would generate a reading of ~365,000

**Conclusion:**
The observed reading of 58,872 is consistent with approximately 10 months of consumption from a zero starting point, not five years. The most probable explanation is meter replacement during the lease period, most likely as part of the ESB Networks smart meter installation programme.

**Implications:**
- Current meter captures approximately 10 months of data only
- Historical consumption patterns for the full lease period require complete billing history from Flogas
- Client's portfolio comparison evidence supports the conclusion that excess consumption predates the current meter installation
