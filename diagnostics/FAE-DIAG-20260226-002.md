## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The Clearspace re-audit revealed deficiencies in the initial analytical pass across five categories: consumption calculation errors (double-counting from reversal misprocessing), financial calculation cascading errors, **data extraction errors (I-SEM rate recorded at 10× below actual)**, misinterpretation of standard billing chain mechanics, and data alignment failures in report generation. All five were identified and corrected through the VeriTech 10 human-in-the-loop verification process, which operated as designed.

**The I-SEM extraction error was identified through arithmetic cross-referencing — the invoice net total proved the rate was incorrect before the source document was re-examined — demonstrating the value of the Step 2/Step 9 cross-validation methodology.**

## KEY LESSONS LEARNED

### I-SEM Cross-Check Methodology
When charge-line rate extraction produces figures that don't reconcile to invoice net total:

**Step 1:** Sum all other verified charge lines
**Step 2:** Calculate residual from net total  
**Step 3:** Derive actual rate from residual ÷ consumption units

This cross-referencing methodology must be implemented as automated validation on every invoice processed.

### Anomaly Separation Logic
Platform must separately evaluate:
- (a) Whether billing process/chain is standard for supplier
- (b) Whether individual events within process contain anomalies

Normal processes can contain abnormal events. Both layers must be assessed independently.
