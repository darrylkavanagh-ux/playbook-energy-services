## 5. METER HISTORY ANALYSIS AND DATA LIMITATIONS

### 5.1 Data Coverage Limitation

The three invoices analysed in this audit cover a period of 94 days (17 October 2025 to 19 January 2026). This represents approximately 5% of the 5-year lease period. No billing data is available for the preceding 4 years and 9 months.

### 5.2 Meter Reading Analysis

**Current Meter Reading:** Z00006629 reads 58,872 as of 19 January 2026 (Customer Read)

**Critical Inconsistency:** At the current verified consumption rate of approximately 208 kWh/day, five years of consumption would produce a reading of approximately 380,000 kWh. The actual reading of 58,872 is fundamentally inconsistent with five years of consumption at the current rate.

### 5.3 Four-Scenario Analysis

**SCENARIO A: Meter reset to zero at lease start (~2021)**
- If 5-year total consumption = 58,872 kWh
- Average daily: 58,872 ÷ 1,825 = 32.3 kWh/day
- Current rate: ~208 kWh/day (6.4x higher)
- First 4.7 years would average: ~21 kWh/day
- **VERDICT: IMPLAUSIBLE** — 21 kWh/day insufficient for commercial office operation

**SCENARIO B: Meter not reset, carried from residential**
- Starting reading: UNKNOWN
- Cannot calculate 5-year consumption without starting point
- Requires complete Flogas billing history
- **VERDICT: POSSIBLE** but unquantifiable without data

**SCENARIO C: Meter replaced during lease (ESB smart meter rollout)**
- At 200 kWh/day: 58,872 ÷ 200 = 294 days
- Installation date: ~March/April 2025
- Aligns with ESB Networks smart meter rollout timeline
- Explains reading without requiring consumption spike
- **VERDICT: MOST PLAUSIBLE** — consistent with all evidence

**SCENARIO D: Consumption grew over time**
- Test: 20 kWh/day for 4 years + 200 kWh/day for 1 year
- Calculation: (20 × 1,460) + (200 × 365) = 102,200 kWh
- Still exceeds actual reading of 58,872
- **VERDICT: DOES NOT FIT THE DATA**

### 5.4 Most Plausible Explanation

The meter was most likely replaced during the lease period, possibly as part of the ESB Networks smart meter rollout programme. At 200 kWh/day, a meter starting from zero would reach 58,872 in approximately 294 days, placing the installation at approximately March/April 2025.

This means the current meter reading captures approximately 10 months of consumption, not the full 5-year lease period.

### 5.5 Implication for Financial Claims

The 50% consumption excess reported by Clearspace across their 27-building portfolio suggests an ongoing issue predating the current meter. However, the exact duration and magnitude of the excess over the full lease cannot be determined from the available invoices or meter readings alone.
