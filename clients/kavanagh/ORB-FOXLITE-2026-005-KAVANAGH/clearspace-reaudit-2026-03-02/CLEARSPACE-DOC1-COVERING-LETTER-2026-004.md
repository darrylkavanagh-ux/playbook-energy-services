We write further to our engagement as independent energy consultants to Clearspace Ltd in respect of the electricity supply at Carmichael House, 60 Lower Baggot Street, Dublin 2 (MPRN: 10002106755, Account: 400020613, Supplier: Flogas Natural Gas Ltd).

We have now completed our forensic audit of the three available invoices covering the period 17 October 2025 to 19 January 2026 (95 days). Our findings are set out in the enclosed One-Page Audit Summary (Document 2).

**Summary of Key Findings:**

1. **Tariff Misclassification (CONFIRMED):** Your premises are billed under the DG5 NSHeat New Business Direct tariff with a residential MCC03 two-register meter configuration. Carmichael House is a commercial office. The Night Storage Heat (NSH) meter has recorded zero consumption on every available invoice. This configuration is inconsistent with the commercial use of the property and requires immediate correction by Flogas.

2. **Consumption Excess (CONFIRMED):** Verified consumption over 95 days is 22,639 kWh (238.3 kWh/day, annualised 86,980 kWh/year). Based on David Clarke's professional assessment across 17 comparable Clearspace commercial office properties in Dublin 2, the expected consumption is approximately 58,000 kWh/year. The excess of approximately 29,000 kWh/year represents a 50% overconsumption at an estimated cost of €8,990–€13,330 per year. An ESB Networks meter test has been requested to determine whether this excess is attributable to a metering fault or an internal electrical issue.

3. **Billing Irregularities (CONFIRMED):** Three invoices were issued within 12 days (9, 12, and 21 January 2026). Invoice 2 (issued 12 January) treated Invoice 1 (due 23 January) as an outstanding balance 11 days before it was due. These practices require explanation from Flogas.

4. **Outstanding Data:** We have formally requested the complete billing history from account opening (November 2023) under GDPR Article 15. Full quantification of the overcharge is not possible until this data is received.
