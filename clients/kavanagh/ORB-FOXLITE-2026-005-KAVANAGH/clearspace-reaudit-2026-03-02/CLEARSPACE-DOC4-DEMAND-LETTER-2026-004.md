## 1. TARIFF MISCLASSIFICATION

**The Facts:**

The Client's premises at Carmichael House, 60 Lower Baggot Street, Dublin 2, D02 KP79 is a commercial office property. It has been billed under the DG5 NSHeat New Business Direct tariff with a residential MCC03 two-register meter configuration since account opening (approximately November 2023).

Our forensic audit of invoices INV1000224513, INV1000231654, and INV1000270263 confirms:

1. The Night Storage Heat (NSH) meter (Z00002768) has recorded **zero consumption** on every available invoice.
2. The MCC03 configuration is a **residential two-register configuration** inconsistent with commercial office use.
3. The NSHeat tariff component is **inapplicable** to a commercial property with no night storage heating.
4. The historical NSH reading of 128,760 kWh (meter Z00002768 × multiplier 20) reflects the property's **former residential use**, not its current commercial use.

**The Legal Position:**

Under the CRU Supplier Handbook 2023, you are obliged to apply the correct tariff classification to each customer's account. The application of a residential tariff configuration to a commercial property constitutes a breach of your regulatory obligations. Under the principle of unjust enrichment (Corporation of Dublin v Building and Allied Trades' Union [1996] 1 IR 468), you are not entitled to retain any benefit arising from the misclassification.

**Our Demand:**

1. Immediately reclassify the account to the correct commercial DG5 tariff without the NSHeat component.
2. Remove the MCC03 residential meter configuration and apply the correct commercial configuration.
3. Provide written confirmation of the reclassification within **14 days** of this letter.
4. Provide a full recalculation of all charges from account opening (November 2023) to present, applying the correct tariff, within **30 days** of this letter.
